# Priceplain + Solvimon Startup Plan

Run date: 21 June 2026

## Executive Summary

Priceplain is a startup idea built around one clear problem: AI founders can now build apps faster than they can understand how those apps should make money.

The specific gap is the step before billing implementation. A founder has an AI app, rough usage costs and a possible customer, but they do not yet know:

- what value metric to charge on;
- how much usage to include in each tier;
- where overages should start;
- whether a free tier will destroy margin;
- which events need to be metered;
- how to turn pricing strategy into billing objects.

Solvimon is the right downstream solution because it is built for metering, hybrid pricing, credits, seats, usage, checkout, payments, quote-to-cash and revenue operations. Priceplain sits one step before Solvimon: it helps a founder design the pricing model, then creates a Solvimon-ready handoff with meters, plans, invoice items, credit policies and implementation notes.

In short:

```text
Priceplain decides what to charge and what to meter.
Solvimon turns that plan into billing, checkout, payments and revenue operations.
```

This is a credible business because the user pain is concrete, the customer is identifiable, the monetisation route is believable, and the product naturally pushes users towards a real billing implementation.

AI apps rarely fit simple flat subscription billing. A real AI product might charge for seats, tokens, generated media, API calls, agent runs, storage, credits, enterprise commits, overages or custom contracts. That is exactly where founders get stuck.

Solvimon is relevant because its public positioning is centred on the same monetisation problems Priceplain models:

- usage-based and hybrid pricing;
- metering;
- credits;
- seats;
- enterprise contracts;
- checkout and payments;
- quote-to-cash;
- revenue recognition;
- MCP and CLI workflows for AI-assisted setup.

Priceplain does not replace Solvimon. It creates the strategic layer that makes Solvimon easier to use.

The relationship is:

| Founder Question | Priceplain Output | Solvimon Execution |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What should I charge for? | Value metric recommendation | Meter setup |
| What pricing tiers should exist? | Free, Starter, Growth, Scale tiers | Plan creation |

The reason Solvimon matters is that pricing is not only a table on a website. Once users start paying, the business needs reliable meters, invoices, credits, payment collection, contract logic and revenue records. Priceplain proves that thinking through these objects early makes the product more likely to become a real business.

## Product Concept

Priceplain is an AI pricing copilot for founders building AI apps.

A founder enters:

- app description;
- target customer;
- value metric;
- active customers;
- monthly usage per customer;
- variable cost drivers;
- fixed monthly costs;
- gross-margin target;
- paid conversion;
- monthly growth;
- monthly churn.

Priceplain returns:

- pricing tiers;
- usage caps;
- overage rates;
- gross-margin checks;
- free-tier risk;
- metering events;
- Solvimon-style handoff JSON;
- revenue and COGS simulation;
- sensitivity analysis;
- startup case and submission pack.

The product is deliberately practical. The founder should leave with a pricing model they can discuss with a co-founder, investor, billing engineer or Solvimon setup flow.

## User Pain

The pain is not "I need a prettier pricing page." The real pain is uncertainty.

Founders do not know if their AI app can survive real usage:

- A generous free tier can attract users but create high model costs.
- A flat subscription can be simple but punish heavy users or undercharge enterprise teams.
- Usage-based pricing can be fair but hard to explain without a clear value metric.
- Credits can work well for AI apps but require careful wallet and burn-down logic.
- Enterprise customers often need custom contracts, commits and invoice detail.

The founder needs help before they commit to checkout, billing logic and public pricing.

## Why Someone Would Pay

The economic argument is simple: wrong pricing costs more than a pricing tool.

Potential willingness to pay comes from:

- avoided margin loss from underpriced usage;
- reduced time spent building billing assumptions manually;
- faster path to checkout and revenue;
- better investor/customer explanation;
- less engineering rework when pricing changes;
- clearer handoff to Solvimon or another billing platform.

Example:

If an AI app has 500 active customers and underestimates usage cost by only £2 per customer per month, that is £1,000 per month of hidden margin damage. A £49 or £99 planning tool is easy to justify if it catches that risk before launch.

Payment is especially believable because the buyer is already in a spending moment. They are preparing to launch, charge customers, choose a billing system or change pricing. Priceplain attaches to an existing business workflow rather than trying to create a new habit from scratch.

## Differentiation

Priceplain is different because it connects four areas that are usually separate:

- pricing strategy;
- AI unit economics;
- revenue simulation;
- billing implementation objects.

Alternatives tend to fall into separate categories:

- generic AI chat prompts can suggest pricing but do not create billing handoff objects;
- billing platforms can execute pricing but expect the business to know what it wants to charge;
- spreadsheets can model revenue but are fragile and not tied to meters or invoices;
- pricing consultants can help, but are too expensive for early builders.

Priceplain's wedge is not "better billing." The wedge is "pricing clarity before billing."

## Solvimon Integration Path

The current MVP creates a Solvimon-ready handoff, not a full production integration.

Current handoff includes:

- value metric;
- meter events;
- plans;
- invoice items;
- credit policies;
- implementation notes;
- compact developer object;
- JSON export.

Next integration step:

1. User generates a Priceplain handoff.
2. User connects a Solvimon sandbox key.
3. Priceplain calls Solvimon or uses Solvimon MCP tooling to create meters and plans.
4. Solvimon creates a checkout link for the selected paid tier.
5. The founder tests checkout with Solvimon test cards.
6. The founder iterates pricing before going live.

This is a strong sponsor story because Priceplain creates demand for the exact billing objects Solvimon supports.
